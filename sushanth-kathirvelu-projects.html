Dynamic Slotting

Monetate Inc. · Enterprise recommendation orchestration

PythonRankingRealtime systemsExperimentation
10% CTR + conversion lift

This initiative focused on replacing static recommendation placement with a dynamic orchestration layer that could combine multiple model outputs in real time and make better placement decisions based on context.

Problem

  • Static placement logic could not adapt well to changing user context.
  • Multiple recommendation models existed, but their outputs needed a stronger coordination layer.
  • The business needed measurable lifts in customer engagement and conversion.

Approach

  • Architected a dynamic slotting algorithm to aggregate and rank results from multiple recommendation systems.
  • Used weighted scoring driven by user behavior and product affinity signals.
  • Supported rollout through staged release and testing in client environments.

Engineering considerations

  • Balanced ranking quality with realtime serving expectations.
  • Worked in a production setting where client impact and rollout risk had to be managed carefully.
  • Connected algorithm design to experimentation so results could be measured on live traffic.

Outcome

  • Increased click-through and conversion by 10%.
  • Drove measurable revenue uplift across enterprise clients.
  • Created a more flexible foundation for future recommendation strategies.

  • Needed ranking logic that could generalize across different personalized surfaces.
  • Required a practical balance between business goals, user experience, and infrastructure constraints.

Outcome

  • Improved recommendation quality and perceived personalization.
  • Increased customer click rate by 20%.
  • Reduced redundant product exposure across modules.

Object Detection in 3D Coral Ecosystem Maps

University of Georgia · Published at ICPR 2022

Computer visionSLAM3D localization
Published research

This research proposed a workflow for turning 2D image detections into accurate 3D coral object localization within ecosystem maps built from multiple image sequences.

Problem

  • Ecological mapping benefits from 3D localization, but many strong detectors operate in 2D image space.
  • A practical method was needed to convert 2D detections into meaningful 3D object representations.
  • The work needed benchmarking against established 3D approaches.

Approach

  • Combined 2D detectors such as Faster R-CNN and RetinaNet with camera calibration and ray tracing.
  • Projected detections into a 3D SLAM-generated map.
  • Benchmarked against Frustum ConvNet and PointNet-style methods.

Engineering considerations

  • The method had to reconcile 2D detection quality with spatial accuracy in 3D.
  • Camera calibration and mapping fidelity were central to the pipeline.
  • The research required balancing novelty, feasibility, and benchmarking rigor.

Outcome

  • Enabled volumetric reef analysis through 3D object localization.
  • Highlighted performance trade-offs relative to established 3D methods.
  • Resulted in publication at the 26th International Conference on Pattern Recognition in 2022.
